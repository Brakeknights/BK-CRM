Brake Caliper Repair & Replacement

Brake calipers are the hydraulic clamps that squeeze your brake pads against the rotors. A sticking caliper causes uneven wear, pulls to one side, and can lead to overheating. A leaking caliper is a brake failure waiting to happen.

We diagnose whether your caliper needs a rebuild or full replacement, then perform the repair on-site with quality parts and a proper brake bleed to restore clean pedal feel.

What’s Included

  • Caliper removal and full inspection
  • Caliper rebuild or replacement (new/remanufactured)
  • Brake bleed and hydraulic pressure check
  • Brake pad inspection & replacement if needed
  • Road-test for even braking

Drum Brake Shoe Replacement

Many vehicles — particularly older models and lighter trucks — still use drum brakes on the rear axle. While drum brakes are durable, the shoes do wear down and the hardware can corrode and seize over time.

Our techs handle complete drum brake service including shoe replacement, drum inspection and resurfacing, wheel cylinder inspection, and full hardware kit replacement.

Brake Hose Replacement

Brake hoses are the flexible lines that carry hydraulic fluid from your hard lines to the calipers. Over time, the rubber degrades from the inside — creating restrictions, bulges, or outright failures that can cause complete brake loss.

Hose damage isn’t always visible until it’s too late. If your vehicle is over 6 years old or you’re experiencing spongy pedal feel or uneven braking, a hose inspection is a smart precaution.

What’s Included

  • Visual and pressure inspection of all brake hoses
  • Replace any cracked, bulging, or degraded hoses
  • Reconnect to hard lines with correct fittings
  • Complete brake system bleed after replacement
  • Test for leaks and proper pressure

Brake Fluid Flush

Brake fluid is hygroscopic — it absorbs moisture from the air over time. As moisture content increases, the fluid’s boiling point drops, which can cause brake fade during hard stopping. Old fluid also causes corrosion inside your calipers, master cylinder, and ABS module.

A complete brake fluid flush replaces all old fluid throughout the system with fresh DOT-spec fluid, restoring full hydraulic performance and protecting expensive brake components.

What’s Included

  • Drain and flush entire brake fluid system
  • Refill with fresh DOT 3 or DOT 4 fluid per spec
  • Bleed all four calipers or wheel cylinders
  • Check master cylinder reservoir condition
  • Inspect brake lines for external leaks
